Installing the Short Stories application
In order to read stories on your iPhone, iPod touch or iPad, you will first need to go to the App Store and download the East of the Web Short Stories app onto your iPhone, iPod touch or iPad. There is no charge for the Short Stories app.
● Installing the app
You can do this directly from your iPhone, iPod touch or iPad:

1. Tap on the App Store icon on the home screen of your iPhone, iPod touch or iPad.

2. Search for ‘East of the Web Short Stories’ to find the app.

3. Follow the installation instructions on the app page.

You can also download the Short Stories app via iTunes on your computer:

Click on the link below and then follow the installation instructions on the app page in iTunes:


● Starting the app
When you have installed the Short Stories app, tap on the Short Stories icon on your iPhone, iPod touch or iPad to start the app and begin downloading stories.
Introduction to the Short Stories application
There are four sections of the Short Stories app:

Your Library - This is where stories that you have downloaded can be found. There are four sections in your Library - 'Stories', 'Collections', 'Authors' and 'Groups'. Tapping on a collection, author or group takes you to the associated stories. Tapping on a story takes you to that story’s text.



Story Text - Tapping on a story in your Library takes you to a story’s text. You can move from page to page of the story by swiping or tapping on the side of the screen. You can also change the story's group as well as text size, justification, color and font.



Info and Settings - Tapping on the small round Information icon at the top right of your Library takes you to a section that allows you to access online help, contact support and change the application's settings.



East of the Web Short Stories - Tapping on the ‘Get Stories’ tab takes you online, where you can browse or search for stories and download them to your Library.


Your Library: adding, removing, sorting and grouping stories and collections
Your Library is where stories that you have downloaded can be found. There are four sections in your Library - 'Stories', 'Collections', 'Authors' and 'Groups'. Each one is accessed by tapping on the tab icon at the bottom of the screen.
● Your Library sections
- The stories section is a list of all the stories in your Library.

- The authors section is a list of all the authors in your Library. Tapping on an author takes you to a list of that author's stories.

- Collections are sets of stories that are intended to be read together (like an 'album' in iTunes). They might all be by a single author (e.g. a collection of Sherlock Holmes stories) or they might be linked by a common theme (e.g. Classic American Stories). Tapping on a collection takes you to a list of the stories in that collection.

- Groups are sets of stories that you create so that you can remember, for instance, which one's you've read or which one's you like (a bit like 'playlists' in iTunes). There are two types of groups:

1. Automatic Groups:
Recently Read - When you tap on a story and go to the story text, it is put in this group. This group contains the ten most recently read stories.
Never Read - Stories that you have never tapped on.
Daily Stories - East of the Web offers a daily story five days a week. This group shows the last five daily stories that are available on your device.

You can choose to automatically download the latest daily story each day when you open the Short Stories app by going to the 'Info and Settings' section and setting 'Auto Download Daily' switch to 'ON'.

You can also manually download the last five daily stories by clicking on the refresh icon (below) at the top right of the Daily Stories group's screen.



2. Your Groups:
You can put a story in one of four groups in 'Your Groups'. You do this by tapping on the relevant group in the Top Controls of the story text:



New - All stories start off in this group when you download them.
Favorites - A group for stories that you really like.
Keep - A group for stories that you want to keep.
Trash - A group for stories that you want to delete.
● Story listing layout


Each story is listed with its title, author and an indication of the number of pages in that story.

Note that the page estimates are based on a normal paperback size page. Depending on the font size you use for reading, the actual number of pages will be larger to a greater or lesser degree.

In addition, each story has:

Amount read indicator - An indicator that shows how far through the story you were when you last stopped reading.
Group indicator - The color of this indicator shows what group the story is in.
● Adding stories to your Library
1. Tap on the ‘Get Stories’ tab. This takes you online to East of the Web Short Stories.



2. Browse or search for stories or collections - see ‘Getting short stories from East of the Web’.

3. When you have chosen the story or collection you want to read, tap on the ‘Download’ button at the bottom right of the story or collection description.



4. The story or stories will begin downloading. You will see a panel at the bottom of your Library indicating this. You can continue to browse, download other stories or read while stories are downloading.

5. When the story has finished downloading it will be added to the list of stories in your Library.
● Removing stories from your Library
1. Swipe your finger to the right over the story, collection or author in your Library that you want to delete. A delete button will appear on the right of the item listing.

2. Tap on this ‘Delete’ button. The story or stories will be removed from your Library.

Alternatively:

1. Tap on the ‘Edit’ button at the top left of your Libary. You will see a circular icon appear to the left of each item listing.

2. Tap on this icon next to the item you want to remove. This will reveal a ‘Delete’ button to the right of the item listing.

3. Tap on this ‘Delete’ button. The story or stories will be removed from you Library.
● Sorting stories or collections in your Library
You can order the list of stories or collections in your Library by tapping on the ‘By Date’ or the ‘By Title’ buttons at the top of the stories or the collections section. These will order them as follows:

By Date: By the date you added the item to your Library
By Title: By the item title
Getting short stories from East of the Web
Tapping on the ‘Get Stories’ tab takes you to East of the Web online, where you can browse or search for stories and download them to your Library.
● Introduction to browsing
Browsing East of the Web Short Stories online works like a web browser. You can tap on links on the page and move back and forward through pages by using the arrow buttons at the top left of the page (these will be dimmed out if there is currently no page to move forward to back to):



Tap on one of your ‘Library’ tab to return to your Library without downloading a story.
● Story listings
Every story and collection is listed with a title, an author, a short description, an age rating, a user rating, an estimate of the number of pages in the story, a link to comments on the story and a ‘Download’ button.



Author Name
Tapping on an author name will take you to that author’s page with a list of all their stories as well as a short biography and links.

Age Ratings
U - Suitable for children of all ages.
PG - Parental Guidance. May contain some elements that parents may not want young children to read.
15 - Contains some strong language, violence or mild sexual content.
18 - Suitable only for adults

Page Estimates
The page estimates are based on a normal paperback size page. Depending on the font size you use for reading, the actual number of pages on your iPhone or iPod touch will be larger to a greater or lesser degree.

Comments
Tapping the number of comments will take you to a page where you can read the comments on that story. Click the back button to return to the previous page.

Download Button
Clicking on the ‘Download’ button will take you back to your Library where the story will start downloading.
● The Home, Genres, Authors, Top 20s, Collections and Search bookmarks
There are six pre-set bookmarks that help you navigate East of the Web online. These can be accessed by tapping the buttons at the bottom of the screen when you are online.

- Home: This will take you to the Short Stories home page. Here you can find today’s daily story, a random story and the current featured stories from across East of the Web.

- Search: This will allow you to search for stories accross East of the Web Short Stories. Tap on the search box to enter text and then tap the ‘Submit’ button. You can search for full or partial titles, authors and even keywords in the plot of the story. Results are returned with the most relevant stories first.

Genres: This will take you to a list of genres that allow you to browse the stories by genre. Each genre has its own page where you can access random and featured stories and browse all the stories in that genre in a number of ways.

Authors: This will take you to a list of all authors on East of the Web. This links to the author pages that list all an author’s stories as well as a brief biography and links.

Top 20s: This gives you quick access to the Top 20 lists in each genre as well as all of East of the Web Short Stories.

Collections: This will take you to lists of the collections available at East of the Web Short Stories. Collections are sets of stories that are intended to be read together (like an Album in iTunes). They might all be by a single author (e.g. a collection of Sherlock Holmes stories) or they might be linked by a common theme (e.g. the 'Classic American Stories' compilation). Tapping on 'Get more details ...' in a collection listing takes you to a list of the stories in that collection.
Reading stories
Tapping on a story title in your Library takes you to that story’s text.
● Introduction to reading stories
To make reading as easy as possible, stories are paged, in much the same way as they would be in a physical book. You can move forward and back through the pages and you can change aspects of the reading format such as text size or orientation of the screen. As you change the format, the story will be repaginated.

Click on the ‘Library’ button at the top left of the page to return to your Library.
● Screen layout


Controls, top - shows the current and maximum page; the slider allows you to skip across pages; the group selector allows you to assign the story to a particular group.
Controls, bottom - allows you to change aspects of the text format.
'Previous page' area - tap in this area to move back a page.
'Next page' area - tap in this area to move forward a page.
'Show/Hide Controls' area - tap to show or hide the Controls.
● Changing pages
You can change the page in one of three ways:

- Swipe anywhere across the screen - right to left to go forward, left to right to go back.

- Tap on the next or back page area on the left or right of the screen.

- You can also skip ahead or back pages by using the slider in the top Controls when the Controls are visible.
● Looking up word definitions
If your device is running iOS 5.0 or higher, you can look up the definition of any work in the story text. Simply tap and hold on the word you want to look up until the definition appears.
● Changing reading format
You access the format Controls by tapping on the Show/Hide Controls area.

The following format controls are available by clicking on the icons in the bottom Controls:

- Text size.
- Serif/sans Serif.
- Text and background color.
- Justified/left aligned
- Lock rotation. This prevents the text from rotating when you change the orientation of the device. Useful for reading in bed!
● Changing the story's group


You can change a story's group by clicking on one of the group selectors in Top Controls. The change of group will be reflected when you return to your Library. The groups are:

New - All stories start off in this group when you download them.
Favorites - A group for stories that you really like.
Keep - A group for stories that you want to keep.
Trash - A group for stories that you want to delete.
● Commenting on a story


You can comment on the current story and read other people's comments on the story by clicking on the comment icon at the top right of the Top Controls.
● Re-pagination
No matter what changes you make to the reading format, you will always instantly be able to view the page you are currently on, and you will always be able to move forward one page at a time.

However, if you change text size or font, sometimes it will take time to recalculate pages before the one you are reading or calculate pages far ahead or behind the one you are currently reading.
● Returning to a story
When you return to a story you will automatically be returned to the page you were reading when you left that story.
Accessing support and changing application settings
Tapping on the small round Information icon at the top right of your Library takes you to a section that allows you to access online help, contact support and change application settings.

There are five areas:

Help: This will take you to an online help area.

About: This will let you know the version of the app you are using.

Contact Support: This will take you to an online area where you can contact East of the Web support via a form.

Terms & Conditions: These are the terms and conditions that you agree to by using the Short Stories app and East of the Web online.

Auto Download Daily: East of the Web offers a daily story five days a week. When this switch is set to 'ON', the application will automatically download the latest daily story when you open the Short Stories app. You can find the last five daily stories that were downloaded in the 'Daily Stories' group in your Library.
